Cosmological singularity: Classical theory (GR) posits that before the Big Bang, the Universe was compressed to a point of infinite density and temperature. In this state, the laws of physics cease to apply, and the concept of "before" does not exist, as time began precisely at this moment.
Quantum cosmology: Suggests that "before" there existed quantum foam—a state where space and time constantly fluctuated. There were no stable particles, only chaotic bursts of energy.
Cyclic model (Roger Penrose's Conformal Cyclic Cosmology): The Universe goes through infinite cycles. Inflation Theory: Before the hot Big Bang, there existed space filled with ultra-dense energy (the inflaton field). This field caused an exponentially rapid expansion, which eventually "melted," giving birth to the matter we know.
False Vacuum: Our Universe could have emerged as a "bubble" in a metastable, high-energy state of another, larger Multiverse.
